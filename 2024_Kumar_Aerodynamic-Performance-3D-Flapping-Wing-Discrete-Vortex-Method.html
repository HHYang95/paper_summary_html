Overview

This paper presents a three-dimensional numerical simulation of flapping rectangular wings using the discrete vortex method (DVM), an inviscid panel method that avoids flow-field grid generation at each time step. Four wing configurations are investigated: rigid, bending, twisting, and bending-twisting coupling (BTC). A novel twisting approach is introduced where the wing rotates about a dynamically adjusted pivot at the root of the leading edge rather than the conventional fixed root axis. Parametric studies cover reduced frequency ($k = 0.1$–$0.6$), aspect ratio ($AR = 1$–$6$), and asymmetric ratio ($AS = 1$–$3$). The BTC mode yields the highest aerodynamic performance, achieving up to 68.75% lift enhancement and optimal propulsive efficiency at $k = 0.3$–$0.4$, $AR = 3$–$4$, and $AS = 2$. This work is directly relevant to Dr. Yang's research on ornithopter aerodynamic modeling and flapping-wing mechanism design, particularly regarding computationally efficient inviscid methods for parametric wing deformation studies.

Introduction

Motivation

  • Micro air vehicles (MAVs) and flapping drones are increasingly important for aerial surveillance, disaster management, and defense applications
  • At small length scales (Re below ~1000), flapping-wing mechanisms outperform fixed-wing aerodynamics due to significant viscous effects causing flow detachment on conventional lifting surfaces
  • Bio-inspired flapping wings offer hovering capability, superior maneuverability, and potentially lower noise emission compared to fixed and rotary wings
  • Unsteady flapping dynamics involves complex phenomena including clap-and-fling, delayed stall, wake capture, and chaotic wake transitions

State of the Art

  • Foundational work on flapping flight dates to Lighthill (1973) and Ellington (1984), with extensive reviews by Tennekes (1997) and Dhawan (1991)
  • Wing deformation's role in aerodynamic improvement is relatively under-studied compared to rigid flapping wings; insect wings are highly flexible structures that deform under aerodynamic and inertial loads
  • Deformation has been shown to improve force generation (Vanella et al., 2009; Mountcastle and Daniel, 2010) and efficiency (Jankauski et al., 2018; Reid et al., 2019)
  • Flexural rigidity variations along the chord (up to two orders of magnitude in hawkmoth Manduca sexta) suggest structural tuning for aerodynamic benefit (Combes and Daniel, 2003)

Gap & Contribution

  • Prior 3D DVM studies of deformable flapping wings with systematic comparison of bending, twisting, and coupled deformation modes are lacking
  • Conventional twisting approaches rotate the wing about a fixed root axis; this study introduces a novel pivot at the leading-edge root that dynamically adjusts during the stroke
  • Comprehensive parametric study across reduced frequency, aspect ratio, and asymmetric ratio for all four deformation modes provides design guidelines for flapping MAVs

Related Works

Computational Studies of Flapping Wings

  • Katz and Plotkin (2001) established the foundational framework for low-speed aerodynamics using discrete vortex methods, providing the unsteady Bernoulli equation formulation for panel pressure computation
  • Ghommem et al. (2012) performed numerical simulations of rectangular flapping wings in forward flight, providing validation benchmarks for the current DVM implementation
  • Stanford and Beran (2010) studied predefined wing deformation using bending and twisting mode shapes with conventional root-axis rotation; their bending mode shape function is adopted in this work
  • Fritz and Long (2004) examined thrust generation mechanisms and the role of trailing-edge vortex dynamics in flapping flight
  • Jaworski and Gordnier (2015) demonstrated thrust augmentation of flapping airfoils at low Re using flexible membranes

Experimental and Bio-Inspired Studies

  • Combes and Daniel (2003) measured spanwise and chordwise flexural stiffness distributions in hawkmoth wings, revealing one-to-two order-of-magnitude gradients along the chord
  • Bomphrey et al. (2017) used smoke visualization to reveal independent leading-edge vortices on each wing pair in mosquito flight at high frequency
  • Dong et al. (2006) reported that for aspect ratios exceeding ~2.55, wingtip vortices detach, leading to efficiency drops
  • Bose and Sarkar (2018) investigated chaotic wake dynamics behind flapping airfoils, explaining the transition from periodicity to chaos through vortex interactions

2D and Prior DVM Studies

  • Kumar and Samanta (2023) developed a 2D DVM simulation for bending and length-varying flapping wings, serving as the direct precursor to this 3D extension
  • Chakravarty and Samanta (2021) applied DVM to simulate 1D flexible filaments mimicking anguilliform swimming in 2D flow
  • Devranjan et al. (2013) presented an early study of flapping flight using discrete vortex method simulations

Materials and Methods

Problem Setup

  • Rectangular wing in forward flight; chord $c = 0.1$ m, aspect ratio $AR = b/c$ varied from 1 to 6
  • Flapping amplitude $\delta_1 = 30°$, initial inclination $\delta_0 = -30°$, angle of attack $\alpha = 2°$, free-stream velocity $U_\infty = 11$ m/s
  • Reynolds number range $Re \approx 380$–$1910$, where inviscid approximation is valid for flapping dynamics
  • Four configurations: rigid, bending (spanwise), twisting (chordwise about leading-edge root pivot), and BTC (bending + twisting coupled)
  • Time step $\Delta t = 0.001$ s; wake vortex shed at $0.3 U_\infty \Delta t$ from the wing tip at each step

Governing Equations

  • Inviscid vorticity transport: $\frac{D\Omega}{Dt} = \Omega \cdot \nabla \vec{u}$, retaining vortex stretching/tilting in 3D while eliminating diffusion
  • Biot-Savart law for induced velocity from a finite vortex segment: $d\vec{v} = \frac{\Gamma}{4\pi} \frac{d\vec{l} \times \vec{r}}{|\vec{r}|^3}$
  • Zero normal velocity boundary condition at collocation points: $\sum_{L=1}^{m} a_{KL} \Gamma_L = RHS_K$, solved as a linear system at each time step
  • Unsteady Bernoulli equation for pressure: $\frac{p_{ref} - p}{\rho} = \frac{Q^2}{2} - \frac{v_{ref}^2}{2} + \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial t}$
  • Panel pressure difference (Katz formulation): $$\Delta p_{ij} = \rho \left[ (\vec{V}_{ij} \cdot \tau_i) \frac{\Gamma_{i,j} - \Gamma_{i-1,j}}{\Delta c_{ij}} + (\vec{V}_{ij} \cdot \tau_j) \frac{\Gamma_{i,j} - \Gamma_{i,j-1}}{\Delta b_{ij}} + \frac{\partial}{\partial t} \Gamma_{i,j} \right]$$
  • Lift coefficient: $C_L = F_L / (0.5 \rho S U_\infty^2)$; Thrust coefficient: $C_T = F_X / (0.5 \rho S U_\infty^2)$
  • Propulsive efficiency: $\eta_p = C_T / C_P$, where $C_P = P / (0.5 \rho S U_\infty^3)$

Wing Kinematics

  • Symmetric flapping: $\delta(t) = \delta_0 + \delta_1 \sin(\omega t)$
  • Asymmetric flapping: piecewise sinusoidal with different downstroke and upstroke speeds, governed by asymmetric ratio $AS$
  • Bending mode shape (from Stanford and Beran, 2010): polynomial function of spanwise coordinate $y/b$
  • Novel twisting: wing rotates about pivot point O at the root of the leading edge (not the conventional root axis), with twist angle varying along span
  • BTC mode: simultaneous bending and twisting deformations superposed

Numerical Implementation

  • Wing discretized into $N \times M$ quadrilateral panels with vortex ring elements; bound vortex at quarter-chord, collocation at three-quarter-chord
  • Lagrangian wake tracking: trailing-edge vortex rings shed and convected at each time step
  • Influence coefficient matrix $[a_{KL}]$ assembled and solved at each time step via matrix inversion
  • MATLAB implementation; simulations run for 30 cycles to achieve near-steady time-averaged values

Validation Strategy

  • DVM code validated against Ghommem et al. (2012) for a rectangular wing in forward flight with flapping motion
  • Grid convergence study performed to determine adequate panel resolution ($N \times M$)
  • Time-averaged lift and thrust converge after approximately 30 cycles (Fig. SI-VI)

Results

Validation Results

  • DVM results show good agreement with the benchmark data of Ghommem et al. (2012) for rigid rectangular wing in forward flight
  • Temporal lift and thrust profiles reproduce the expected sinusoidal behavior with peak magnitudes at wing mid-position during each half-stroke

Deformation Mode Comparison

  • At $k = 0.6$ (symmetric), lift coefficients: rigid $C_L = 0.805$, bending $C_L = 0.916$ (+13.78%), twisting $C_L = 1.25$ (+55.25%), BTC $C_L = 1.358$ (+68.75%)
  • At $k = 0.6$ (asymmetric, $AS = 2$), lift coefficients: rigid $C_L = 1.36$, bending $C_L = 1.51$ (+11%), twisting $C_L = 1.95$ (+43.3%), BTC $C_L = 2.15$ (+53.57%)
  • Bending mode provides modest lift improvement with relatively small thrust enhancement; twisting and BTC modes yield substantially higher lift and thrust
  • BTC wing achieves the highest pressure levels at the leading edge across all configurations, generating the strongest aerodynamic forces

Reduced Frequency Effects

  • Both $C_L$ and $C_T$ increase monotonically with reduced frequency $k$ from 0.1 to 0.6 for all four modes
  • Propulsive efficiency $\eta_p$ increases with $k$ up to a maximum in the range $k = 0.3$–$0.4$, then decreases monotonically at higher frequencies
  • At higher $k$, the unsteady term $\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\Gamma_{i,j}$ becomes dominant in determining aerodynamic forces
  • Higher $k$ produces more energetic wake patterns with stronger vortex shedding from the trailing edge; wake vortex rings are located closer to the wing surface

Aspect Ratio Effects

  • Propulsive efficiency increases continuously up to $AR \approx 3$–$4$, then decreases with further AR increase for all four modes
  • For $AR > 2.55$, wingtip vortices begin to detach, consistent with findings of Dong et al. (2006)
  • Higher AR wings experience greater tip velocities and larger deformations, contributing to improved aerodynamic performance within the optimal range

Asymmetric Ratio Effects

  • Asymmetric flapping ($AS > 1$) produces notably higher time-averaged aerodynamic forces compared to symmetric flapping
  • Propulsive efficiency peaks at $AS = 2$ for all four wing configurations, then decreases due to increased power requirements at higher $AS$
  • Higher downstroke speed generates larger and stronger trailing vortices, directly enhancing thrust production

Results Interpretation

Vortex Dynamics Mechanisms

  • Twisted and BTC wings maintain higher-strength vortices near the trailing edge, directly enhancing circulation by Kelvin's theorem: $\frac{D\Gamma_{wing}}{Dt} + \frac{D\Gamma_{wake}}{Dt} = 0$
  • The novel leading-edge pivot twisting creates a spanwise-varying angle of attack, producing stronger pressure differentials and more concentrated wingtip vortices
  • Chaotic wake behavior observed in twisted and BTC modes arises from non-uniform vorticity distribution along the span induced by the twisting motion
  • At high $k$, the negative vortex above rigid wings produces a lift penalty, while deformable wings (especially BTC) position positive vortices beneath the wing for consistently higher average lift

Physical Interpretation of Pressure Distribution

  • BTC wings exhibit the highest positive pressure at the leading edge, facilitating flow attachment but also risking adverse pressure gradients at extreme conditions
  • The third term $\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\Gamma_{i,j}$ in the pressure equation becomes dominant at high reduced frequencies, explaining the nonlinear increase in aerodynamic forces
  • The propulsive efficiency peak at $k = 0.3$–$0.4$ reflects a balance between increasing force production and the rising power cost of faster flapping

Limitations

  • Inviscid DVM neglects viscous effects including boundary layer separation and transition; validity is limited to the moderate-Re range ($Re \approx 380$–$1910$)
  • Structural deformation is prescribed (kinematic), not computed via fluid-structure interaction; the feedback of aerodynamic loads on wing shape is not captured
  • Wing morphing power and inertial power input for flapping are neglected, which is a reasonable assumption for forward flight but may not hold for hovering
  • Only rectangular planform is studied; more realistic wing shapes (tapered, elliptical) are not considered

Conclusion
  • BTC (bending-twisting coupling) mode is the most effective deformation strategy, achieving up to 68.75% lift enhancement (symmetric) and 53.57% (asymmetric) over rigid wings at $k = 0.6$
  • Optimal aerodynamic performance is achieved with reduced frequency $k = 0.3$–$0.4$, aspect ratio $AR = 3$–$4$, and asymmetric ratio $AS = 2$
  • The novel leading-edge root pivot for wing twisting is more effective than conventional root-axis rotation in generating aerodynamic forces
  • DVM provides a computationally efficient framework for 3D unsteady flapping simulations without requiring flow-field grid generation at each time step
  • Twisting mode alone provides significant improvements (55.25% lift increase), making it a practical intermediate option when full BTC is not feasible
  • Future work should incorporate fluid-structure interaction (FSI) to capture the feedback between aerodynamic loads and structural deformation
  • The parametric design guidelines (optimal $k$, $AR$, $AS$) provide a framework for flapping MAV design optimization
