Overview

This paper develops an integrative ornithopter flight simulator coupling a modal-based flexible multibody dynamics solver (Craig-Bampton method) with a semiempirical reduced-order flapping-wing aerodynamic model (Modified Strip Theory Improved) through loosely coupled fluid-structure interaction. Applied to a bioinspired ornithopter with 540 mm wingspan, the simulator reveals that trimmed level flight exhibits stable limit-cycle oscillations uniquely determined by flapping frequency, with mean body pitch attitude inversely proportional to flapping frequency and forward flight speed linearly increasing with it. The FSI-induced phase delays between imposed wing motion and aeroelastic response are shown to be critical for thrust generation and passive flight stability.

Introduction

Problem Context

  • Ornithopter flight dynamics involve complex coupling of unsteady low Reynolds number aerodynamics, wing kinematics, and structural flexibility
  • Unlike insect-scale MAVs, the flapping frequency time scale of larger ornithopters is close to the vehicle's dynamic mode time scales, making cycle-averaging methods ineffective
  • Previous studies used quasi-static or quasi-steady aerodynamic models with rigid-body dynamics and prescribed wing motions, neglecting FSI effects

Research Gap and Objectives

  • Literature on ornithopter flight dynamics considering wing flexibility and FSI was essentially nonexistent
  • This work aims to build an integrative flight simulator with FSI capability and investigate frequency-dependent trim flight characteristics
  • The simulator enables monitoring of all flight state variables, overcoming limitations of physical flight tests with restricted sensors and endurance

Related Works

Flapping-Wing Aerodynamics

  • DeLaurier (1993) proposed the Modified Strip Theory (MST) for flapping-wing aerodynamics, validated through manned ornithopter flight tests
  • Kim et al. (2008, 2011) improved MST for larger amplitude motions at low Reynolds numbers, adding a dynamic stall regime with three flow classifications
  • Shyy et al. (2010) reviewed progress showing flexible wings achieve higher thrust-to-power ratios than rigid counterparts

Ornithopter Flight Dynamics

  • Dietl and Garcia (2008) studied LCO stability using quasi-static aerodynamics with rigid flapping wings and prescribed motions
  • Grauer and Hubbard (2009) experimentally demonstrated oscillatory flight dynamics and simulated ornithopter flight with simplified quasi-steady aerodynamics
  • Pfeiffer et al. (2009) conducted FSI-based flight dynamics simulation for a single flapping frequency using a predecessor of the present simulator

Materials and Methods

Structural Modeling

  • Quarter-ellipse wing shape with 540 mm wingspan, aspect ratio 5.58, total mass 79.92 g
  • 1857 finite elements in ANSYS: BEAM4 for carbon stiffeners, SHELL63 for Ripstop nylon skin (6 DOFs per node)
  • Craig-Bampton component mode synthesis: 25 fixed-interface normal modes for main wing, 3 for tail wing
  • Flexible multibody dynamics solved via MSC.ADAMS with time-varying mass matrix and Gear stiff integrator

Aerodynamic Modeling

  • Main wing: MST Improved with 27 aerodynamic strips, three flow regimes (attached, dynamic stall, post-stall), and dynamic stall criterion
  • Tail wing: refined nonaffine aerodynamic model with effective angle of attack $\alpha^*$ incorporating forward speed, vertical velocity, pitch rate, and tail deflection rate
  • Tail-wing lift: $L_{tw} = \frac{1}{2}\rho U \hat{V}_{0.25\bar{c}_{tw}} \cdot S_{tw} \cdot C^*_{L,tw}(\alpha^*)$ with finite wing correction

FSI and Trim Search

  • Loosely coupled FSI with 5 ms time step and up to 10 Newton-Raphson iterations per step (1% error bound)
  • Flapping motion: $\Phi = \Phi_{\text{MEAN}} + \Phi_1 \sin(2\pi f t)$ with mean angle 10 deg, amplitude 35 deg, frequency 6.5--16 Hz
  • Trim criteria: bounded state variables with converging mean values over successive flapping periods (weak convergence)

Results

FSI Phase Delay Effects

  • At 12 Hz: FSI-on wing lags 56 deg in wingtip bending; FSI-off wing leads by 4 deg relative to imposed flapping motion
  • Wing twisting at 19th AST: FSI-on leads by 65 deg, FSI-off leads by 160 deg, yielding a net 91-deg FSI-induced delay
  • The 91-degree phase shift is critical for generating positive thrust during the 4/10 to 5/10 flapping cycle

Limit-Cycle Oscillation and Trim

  • All longitudinal state variables exhibit stable LCO at trimmed conditions, uniquely determined by flapping frequency
  • LCO phase portraits recover to original orbits after external force and moment disturbances at 12 Hz
  • Phase portrait complexity decreases at higher frequencies: double-loop trajectories transition to single-loop patterns

Frequency-Dependent Trim Characteristics

  • Mean forward flight speed increases nearly linearly: ~12 m/s at 6.5 Hz to ~24 m/s at 16 Hz
  • Mean body pitch attitude decreases inversely: ~5 deg at 6.5 Hz to ~3 deg at 16 Hz
  • Peak-to-peak pitch oscillation generally decreases with increasing flapping frequency up to 14 Hz
  • Tail-wing aeroelastic response shows amplification near 9--10 Hz and 14--15 Hz from higher harmonic forcing

Results Interpretation

Physical Mechanisms

  • FSI-induced phase delay between imposed flapping and aeroelastic response is the key mechanism for proper thrust generation
  • The passive flexible tail wing acts as a nonlinear feedback controller: increased tail-wing response correlates with decreased body pitch oscillation
  • Tail-wing first bending natural frequency (31.75 Hz) is sufficiently above operational range, but higher harmonics in pitch LCO can excite resonance

Comparison with Avian Flight

  • Mean flight characteristics parallel avian flight: body pitch attitude and tail angle decrease with increasing airspeed/flapping frequency
  • Unlike birds which modulate wingbeat amplitude and downstroke velocity, the ornithopter uses symmetric flapping with frequency as the sole control parameter
  • The functional relationships between flapping frequency and trim states validate bioinspired design principles

Conclusion

Key Findings

  • Trim flight conditions are uniquely determined by flapping frequency (frequency-dependent trim characteristics)
  • Stable LCO provides inherent robustness to external disturbances under continuous flapping without active control
  • Mean flight dynamics show systematic functional relationships with flapping frequency: pitch attitude inversely proportional, flight speed linearly proportional

Future Directions

  • Extension to lateral-directional dynamics and active control synthesis exploiting LCO characteristics
  • Experimental validation of frequency-dependent trim relationships across the full 6.5--16 Hz operational envelope
  • Application to systematic ornithopter design for UAV mission profiles and higher-fidelity aerodynamic coupling
