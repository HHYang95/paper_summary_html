Overview

This paper presents a scalable computational framework combining the lattice Boltzmann method (LBM), multi direct-forcing immersed boundary method (IBM), and topology-confined block refinement for simulating complex flows past 2D and 3D flapping wings. The framework is validated against experimental and computational references. For 3D bio-inspired flapping wings at $Re_t = 300$, the Liutex vortex identification method is applied for the first time to classify rotational vortex structures into LEV, tip vortex, TEV, root vortex, and a newly identified U-shaped vortex. The U-shaped vortex formation correlates strongly with lift coefficient jumps, where the maximum $C_L$ at $St = 0.4$ is four times higher than at $St = 0.2$.

Introduction

Motivation

  • Moving boundaries of complex geometries are prevalent in insect and bird flight, rotorcraft, MAVs, and AUVs
  • The lattice Boltzmann method offers simpler algorithms with natural parallelism by solving the kinetic equation instead of the Poisson equation required by conventional CFD
  • The association of instantaneous coherent vortex structures with aerodynamic forces and flow separation during flapping flight has not been fully clarified

State of the Art

  • LBM has grown as a parallel CFD alternative using the BGK collision model and recovering incompressible Navier-Stokes equations via Chapman-Enskog analysis
  • The IBM by Peskin (1977) and multi direct-forcing variant eliminate artificial constants for boundary treatment
  • First LBM-IBM coupling by Feng and Michaelides (2004); Kang and Hassan established interface schemes for moving boundaries
  • Third-generation Liutex vortex identification by Liu et al. (2018) isolates rotational motion without shear contamination

Gap & Contribution

  • Existing studies focus on force measurements; correlation between instantaneous coherent structures and aerodynamic forces remains unclear
  • This paper combines IB-LBM with topology-confined block refinement and applies Liutex identification for the first time to 3D flapping wing flows
  • The U-shaped vortex structure and its correlation with lift generation mechanisms are newly identified

Materials and Methods

LBM Formulation

  • BGK collision with discrete equilibrium distribution: $\Lambda_i^{e} = w_i \rho\left[1 + \frac{\mathbf{c}_i \cdot \mathbf{u}}{c_s^2} + \frac{(\mathbf{c}_i \cdot \mathbf{u})^2}{2c_s^4} - \frac{\mathbf{u}^2}{2c_s^2}\right]$
  • Lattice speed of sound $c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}$; kinematic viscosity $\nu = \Delta t\, c_s^2(\tau - 1/2)$
  • Macroscopic variables recovered from moments of the distribution function: $\rho = \sum_i \Lambda_i$, $\rho\mathbf{u} = \sum_i \mathbf{c}_i \Lambda_i$

Multi Direct-Forcing IBM

  • Rigid geometry surfaces represented by Lagrangian marker points immersed in Eulerian grid with spacing $|r_j - r_k| = \Delta x$
  • No-slip condition enforced iteratively with 5 iterations per time step for optimal accuracy-efficiency balance
  • Discrete delta functions used for interpolation between Eulerian and Lagrangian grid systems

Topology-Confined Block Refinement

  • Domain partitioned into square blocks with variable refinement levels; parallelized via MPI + OpenMP
  • Load balancing by space-filling curves; recursive synchronous algorithm for nonuniform-grid interface interaction
  • 3D domain: $x/\bar{c} \in [-5, 10]$, $y/\bar{c} \in [-2.5, 6.5]$, $z/\bar{c} \in [-3.5, 3.5]$; finest $\Delta x = \bar{c}/48$ (~27M cells)

Wing Model and Kinematics

  • Wing morphology from Thielicke and Stamhuis: root chord $1.14\bar{c}$, tip chord $0.57\bar{c}$, span $2.74\bar{c}$; NACA 4-digit cross sections
  • Pitch: $\alpha(t^*) = \alpha_0 \sin(2\pi f t^*)$ (upstroke only, zero during downstroke); stroke: $\Phi(t^*) = \phi_0 \sin(2\pi f t^* - \pi/2)$
  • Parameters: $\alpha_0 = 45°$, $\phi_0 = 30°$, $Re_t = 300$, $St = 0.2, 0.3, 0.4$

Results

2D Validation

  • Pitch-up flat plate ($Re = 1000$): $C_L$ reaches critical value of $+2.6$ at $\alpha \approx 5°$; LEV/TEV agree with CFD, FLVM, and LDVM references
  • NACA 0018 pitching ($\alpha_0 = 64°$, $k = 0.22$): Lift time history collapses with Theodorsen's theory and experimental data of Otomo et al.
  • 2D flapping plate: $\bar{C}_L = 0.6$, $\bar{C}_D = -0.34$; LEV-TEV dipole pairs and vortex shedding consistent with references

3D Vortex Structure Classification

  • Q-criterion reveals LEV, TEV, TV, and RV; tube-shaped TEV connects TV and RV forming closed starting/stopping vortex structure
  • Liutex identification isolates rotational cores: high $|R|$ in LEV, TEV, TV without RV at all frequencies
  • At $St = 0.2$: Only wingtip vortex core visible; at $St \geq 0.3$: U-shaped vortex forms at $0.5T^*$

U-Shaped Vortex and Lift Correlation

  • U-shaped vortex (LEV + TV + TEV) forms at mid-downstroke; its formation correlates with lift coefficient jumps
  • Maximum $C_L$ at $St = 0.4$ is four times higher than at $St = 0.2$; breakdown from $0.75T^*$ drives lift decrease
  • Transverse spanwise velocity drives LEV separation; TEV disconnects due to helical tip vortex motion
  • Helicity analysis: LEV (clockwise) and TEV (counterclockwise) opposite rotations manifest breakdown; LEV maintains connection due to stronger vortex strength

Results Interpretation

Physical Mechanisms

  • The LEV forms above the upper wing surface and grows asymmetrically, stronger near wingtip due to local boundary shear layer along the leading edge
  • Maximum transverse flow occurs at mid-downstroke when wingtip velocity is maximum, coinciding with LEV separation from swept wing surfaces
  • TEV breaks from U-shaped vortex while LEV does not, indicating stronger vortex strength of LEV at $St = 0.3$ and $0.4$

Resolution and Convergence

  • Kolmogorov length scale: $\Delta/\eta < 3.2$ (max), mean $= 0.67$; 96.5% of domain satisfies $\Delta/\eta < 1$
  • Kolmogorov time scale: $\Delta t/\tau_k$ mean $\approx 0.005$, below the 0.0205 threshold
  • Grid convergence confirmed across three resolutions ($\bar{c}/40$, $\bar{c}/48$, $\bar{c}/56$); near-linear parallel speedup

Conclusion
  • A scalable IB-LBM framework with topology-confined block refinement is validated for 2D and 3D flapping wing aerodynamics
  • Liutex identification applied for the first time to 3D flapping wings classifies rotational structures into LEV, TV, TEV, RV, and U-shaped vortex
  • U-shaped vortex forms at $St \geq 0.3$ and its formation correlates strongly with lift coefficient jumps; maximum $C_L$ at $St = 0.4$ is four times that at $St = 0.2$
  • TEV disconnects from U-shaped vortex due to helical tip vortex motion; LEV maintains connection due to stronger vortex strength
  • Limitations: Restricted to $Re_t = 300$ (low Re); rigid wing with prescribed kinematics; only three Strouhal numbers investigated
  • Future: Higher Re with MRT/LES turbulence models; flexible wing FSI; additional kinematic parameter studies
