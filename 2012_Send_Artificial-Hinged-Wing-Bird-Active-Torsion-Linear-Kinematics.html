Overview

This paper introduces SmartBird, an artificial hinged-wing bird developed by Festo's Bionic Learning Network, featuring two novel concepts in biologically-inspired flight: active torsion and partially linear kinematics. The active torsion mechanism uses a servomotor at the outer wing tip to prescribe a torsion shape function that transitions the angle of incidence between positive and negative values within a short fraction of the flapping period, with the angle held constant between turning points. The partially linear kinematics concept replaces conventional harmonic pitching with a trapezoidal waveform inspired by flying locust observations. Tethered flight measurements demonstrate aerodynamic efficiencies $\eta_{\text{aero}}$ of 60–80%, while free flight achieves sustained horizontal flight at $u_0 = 4.7$ m/s with 18 W total power at $Re \approx 80{,}000$. Both thin-plate theory and 2D Euler code confirm the benefits of partially linear kinematics over harmonic torsion.

Introduction

Historical Context of Flapping-Wing Flight

  • Flapping wings produce thrust without rotating parts by coupling lift and thrust generation through coupled bending and torsional motion
  • The physics traces back to Birnbaum (1924), discovered during airplane flutter research; flutter and flapping-wing propulsion share the same mathematical description
  • High plunging at small pitching produces thrust; low plunging with high pitching extracts energy; the transition domain achieves nearly 90% energy conversion
  • Early ornithopter work by Lippisch (before 1930) and Leonardo da Vinci's hinged-wing designs represent historical milestones in human-built flappers

Motivation and Design Philosophy

  • Primary motivation: better understanding of coupled bending-torsion motion and its optimization for technical applications
  • SmartBird serves as a technology carrier and demonstrator for both thrust production and renewable energy extraction via flapping wings
  • Energy extraction from airstreams using flapping wings was investigated by McKinney and DeLaurier (1981), later extended to the stroke-wing engine up to $150$ kW installed power

Aerodynamic Foundation

  • 3D coupled bending-torsion reduces to 2D coupled pitching $\alpha(t)$ and plunging $h(t)$; the amplitude ratio and phase shift are the two governing parameters
  • In typical flapping flight, plunge leads pitch by $90°$ with maximum positive torsion during upstroke
  • The torsion shape function introduces a third control feature transforming harmonic torsion into partially linear motion

Materials and Methods

SmartBird Mechanical Design

  • Wing: two-part inner spar + trapezoidal hinge (1:3 transmission ratio) + outer wing spar; inner wing generates lift, outer wing generates thrust
  • Specifications: span $b = 2$ m, planform area $A = 0.5$ m$^2$, mean chord $c = 0.25$ m, weight $m = 0.48$ kg including battery
  • Active torsion via servomotor at outer wing tip twisting wing against spar via the outermost rib
  • External rotor motor with two-stage spur gearing (1:45 speed reduction); three Hall sensors for absolute wing position; crank mechanism without dead center for smooth flight
  • Head and fuselage movable by two electric drives for aerodynamic body bending and weight displacement

Active Torsion and Kinematics

  • Torsion shape function defined by amplitude, turn beginning/end, slope, and phase shift relative to plunge motion
  • Three kinematic modes compared: partially linear (trapezoidal), harmonic (sinusoidal), and ideally linear (sawtooth)
  • Control parameters interactively adjustable during flight via bi-directional ZigBee radio communication

Experimental Apparatus (ANIPROP RL3)

  • Carousel test stand: model moves against air at rest; inner boom $L = 2.17$ m, outer boom support $d_V + h_S = 1.25$ m; max speed $> 10$ m/s
  • Force sensor ($\pm 50$ N), bar-coded disk (180 bars, 360 positions/rotation), nine slip rings for data and power
  • Aerodynamic angle $\beta_{\text{aero}} = 90°$ at rest, $0°$ for 100% lift in horizontal flight

Efficiency Methodology

  • Total efficiency: $\eta_{\text{tot}} = -P_T / (P_{\text{el}} - P_{0,\text{sv}})$; thrust power: $P_T = (F_{\text{SM}} - F_{\text{tow}}) \cdot (R_f / R_{\beta,M}) \cdot u_0$
  • Electromechanical efficiency $\eta_{\text{em}}$ via dynamometrical brake; aerodynamic efficiency $\eta_{\text{aero}} = \eta_{\text{tot}} / \eta_{\text{em}}$

Results

Tethered and Free Flight Performance

  • Aerodynamic efficiency $\eta_{\text{aero}}$ reached 60–80% in tethered flight; highest values at specific speed-flapping parameter combinations
  • Servo motor bias power $P_{0,\text{sv}} \approx 5$ W; earlier models achieved similar efficiency but insufficient thrust for free flight
  • Free flight: average velocity $u_0 = 4.7$ m/s, total power $P_{\text{el}} = 18$ W, net power 13 W
  • Thrust force $F_T = 0.7$ N; thrust coefficient $c_T = 0.1$; lift coefficient $c_L = 0.7$; $c_L/c_D \approx 7$
  • Reynolds number $Re \approx 80{,}000$; reduced frequency $\omega^* = 0.3$–$0.45$

Sensitivity and Control

  • Very sensitive dependence of thrust generation on torsion shape function parameters observed in flight tests
  • Free flight required even lower electric power than tethered measurements indicated
  • Real-time optimization of control parameters enabled by on-board microcontroller and ZigBee telemetry

Numerical Predictions

  • Thin-plate theory predicts high-efficiency range bounded by thrust generation and active torsion requirements; amplitude ratio $\lambda = h_0 / (\alpha_0 \cdot c/2)$ governs efficiency map
  • 2D Euler code for NACA 7412: design point at $\eta_{\text{aero}} = 0.8$, $\kappa = 100°$; partially linear achieves same efficiency at smaller amplitude ratio than harmonic motion
  • Broad efficiency plateau from CFD not reproduced in flight tests; experimental sensitivity closer to thin-plate theory narrow range

Results Interpretation

  • Active torsion enables operation within the narrow high-efficiency corridor predicted by unsteady aerodynamic theory through direct control of amplitude ratio and phase relationship
  • The trapezoidal torsion waveform maintains constant angle of incidence during productive stroke phases, optimizing flow utilization for thrust generation
  • Partially linear kinematics reduces required pitch amplitude for a given efficiency target because constant-angle plateaus maintain favorable flow conditions for a larger fraction of the cycle
  • The discrepancy between broad CFD efficiency plateau and narrow experimental sensitivity suggests that viscous effects and flow separation constrain the operational envelope more than inviscid theory predicts
  • Precise absolute data of wing torsion and bending in free flight remain unknown; measurement requires continuous spatial tracking of the entire flying bird
  • The suction force from thin-plate theory originates from the first 3% of chord and tends to disappear for increasing lift, representing a critical issue in CFD validation
  • SmartBird implements only two of three aerodynamic DOFs (plunging and torsion but not lagging), suggesting room for further optimization
  • The proximity of propulsion and energy extraction regimes means large birds may fly near the domain of almost complete energy conversion (up to 90%)

Conclusion

  • SmartBird demonstrates successful sustained free flight using active torsion and partially linear kinematics inspired by locust flight observations
  • Tethered flight yields $\eta_{\text{aero}}$ of 60–80% on the ANIPROP RL3 test stand, confirming theoretical predictions for coupled pitching-plunging propulsion
  • Free flight achieves $u_0 = 4.7$ m/s with 18 W total power, $c_L/c_D \approx 7$ at $Re \approx 80{,}000$
  • Thin-plate theory and 2D Euler code confirm partially linear kinematics reduces required pitch amplitude compared to harmonic motion while maintaining high propulsive efficiency
  • Torsion shape function provides real-time in-flight control over critical parameters governing thrust generation
  • Sensitive dependence of thrust on torsion parameters is consistent with the narrow high-efficiency domain from thin-plate theory
  • Future directions include incorporating the lagging DOF and applying flapping wings to renewable energy extraction
