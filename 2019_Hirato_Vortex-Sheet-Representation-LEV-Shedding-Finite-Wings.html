Overview

This paper presents a low-order method for modeling leading-edge vortex (LEV) formation on finite wings by augmenting an unsteady vortex-lattice method (UVLM) with a vortex-sheet representation. The two-dimensional leading-edge suction parameter (LESP) concept is extended to three-dimensional flows, using the criticality of leading-edge suction to determine spanwise LEV onset and nonuniform growth along the wing span. The LEV is modeled as a vortex sheet composed of vortex rings shed from upstream of the leading edge, connected to the bound-vortex sheet through a pseudovortex ring construct. Three AR = 6 wings with SD7003 airfoil sections undergoing pitch ramp motions at Re = 20,000 are tested against RANS CFD, showing broad agreement in LEV structure and reasonable agreement in aerodynamic loads, with approximately three orders of magnitude computational speedup.

Introduction

Research Motivation

  • Leading-edge vortices (LEVs) are ubiquitous in nature (insect/bird flight, autorotating seeds) and engineering (rotorcraft, wind turbines, delta wings, MAVs), generating low-pressure suction-side regions that augment lift
  • LEV advection causes rapid pitching moment variation, making accurate prediction of LEV initiation and development critical for engineering applications
  • Predicting LEV formation on finite wings remains a key challenge, as most existing low-order models are limited to two-dimensional flows

Gaps in Prior Work

  • Two-dimensional LEV models (conformal mapping, discrete-vortex methods) are successful but their 3D extensions are limited
  • Delta wing models assume steady conical vortex structures, unsuitable for general wing geometries in unsteady motion
  • Existing 3D models idealize the LEV as single vortex filaments rather than vortex sheets, limiting physical accuracy
  • Conventional LEV models assume continuous shedding or ad hoc triggers; the physics-based LESP criterion of Ramesh et al. (2014) has been restricted to 2D

Present Contribution

  • First extension of the LESP concept from 2D airfoils to 3D finite wings within a UVLM framework
  • The model predicts spanwise location of LEV onset and the nonuniform growth of the LEV sheet along the span
  • Validation against RANS CFD for three wing configurations (unswept, tip-twisted, swept) undergoing pitch ramp motions

Materials and Methods

UVLM Framework and Vortex-Ring Discretization

  • Wing surface discretized into bound-vortex rings with front edges at quarter-chord and control points at three-quarter-chord of each panel
  • Velocity field computed via Biot-Savart law with Lamb-Oseen viscous core model to avoid singularities: $$v_r = \frac{\Gamma}{4\pi} \frac{r_s \times r_e}{|r_s \times r_e|^2}(|r_s| + |r_e|)\left(1 - \frac{r_s \cdot r_e}{|r_s||r_e|}\right)\left\{1 - e^{-(r/r_c)^2}\right\}$$
  • TEV rings shed at each time step to satisfy Kelvin condition: $\Gamma^n_W = \Gamma^{n-1}_{b,i_{\max}}$

LESP Extension to 3D

  • LESP defined as leading Fourier coefficient $A_0(t)$ in the chordwise bound circulation expansion: $$\gamma(\theta, t) = 2U_\infty \left[\frac{A_0(t)(1+\cos\theta)}{\sin\theta} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n(t)\sin(n\theta)\right]$$
  • For UVLM strips, instantaneous LESP related to the frontmost bound-vortex ring strength through an integral relationship with cotangent weighting
  • LEV formation triggers when LESP exceeds $\text{LESP}_{\text{crit}}$ determined from 2D CFD for the given airfoil/Re combination

LEV Sheet and Pseudovortex Ring

  • LEV rings placed at one-third distance from leading edge to last LEV: $x_{L,n-1} = \tfrac{2}{3}x_{i,\text{L.E.}} + \tfrac{1}{3}x_{L,n-2}$ following Ansari et al.
  • Pseudovortex ring of strength $\Gamma^n_L$ connects LEV sheet to bound-vortex sheet, ensuring zero vortex filament at geometric leading edge
  • Extended Kelvin condition with LEV shedding: $\Gamma^n_W = \Gamma^{n-1}_{b,i_{\max}} + \Gamma^{n-1}_L$

Aerodynamic Load Calculation

  • Normal forces from unsteady Bernoulli equation; potential difference during LEV shedding: $\phi_u - \phi_l = \Gamma^n_L + \Gamma_{b,x}$
  • Leading-edge suction force: $F_S = \pi \rho c U_\infty^2 A_0^2$
  • Total lift: $C_L = C_N \cos\alpha + C_S \sin\alpha$, combining normal and suction force contributions

Test Cases and CFD Reference

  • Three AR = 6 constant-chord wings with SD7003 airfoil at Re = 20,000: unswept, 10 deg tip twist, and swept configurations
  • Pitch ramp 0–45 deg with reduced pitch rate $K = \dot{\alpha}c/(2U_\infty) = 0.3$
  • $\text{LESP}_{\text{crit}} = 0.27$ from 2D CFD of SD7003 airfoil
  • RANS CFD (REACTMB-INS) with Spalart–Allmaras turbulence model on ~35 million cell meshes

Results

LEV Structure Predictions

  • Case 1 (unswept): LEV initiates at the wing root where LESP peaks, then spreads outboard; rolled-up sheet shape agrees broadly with CFD iso-Q contours, though UVLM predicts slightly slower spanwise progression
  • Case 2 (tip twist): LEV onset at midspan ($y \approx 0.5\, b/2$) matching LESP peak; characteristic bulge near $y \approx 0.75\, b/2$ due to higher outboard incidence reproduced by both methods
  • Case 3 (swept): Tip-dominated LEV formation with conical structure reminiscent of delta wing shedding, caused by motion-induced upwash at the wingtip from pitch-up about the root quarter-chord
  • UVLM captures full LEV evolution sequence: onset, spanwise progression, initial rollup, and well-developed vortical structure on the upper surface

Aerodynamic Load Comparisons

  • Apparent mass spikes at pitch start/stop captured by UVLM but overpredicted relative to CFD
  • Pitching moment $C_M$ with LEV shows excellent agreement with CFD between LEV onset and end of pitch-up
  • Lift coefficient $C_L$ with LEV-on shows reduced lift increase rate; CFD shows higher slope during this phase
  • After pitch-up ceases ($\alpha = 45°$ held), LEV-on model predicts decreasing $C_L$ and $C_M$ as the LEV continues to evolve

Discrepancy Sources

  • Underestimation of leading-edge suction force $F_S$ following LEV onset — major contributor to $C_L$ at high $\alpha$
  • Absence of separated tip vortex model affecting outboard downwash distribution
  • Delayed spanwise LEV growth leading to underestimated vortex lift
  • Attached-flow assumption does not model trailing-edge separation on the suction surface

Results Interpretation

  • The LESP-based trigger successfully predicts geometry-dependent LEV onset locations: root (unswept), midspan (twisted), wingtip (swept) — demonstrating that the spanwise LESP distribution encodes the combined effects of geometry, twist, sweep, and motion kinematics
  • A single $\text{LESP}_{\text{crit}}$ value from one 2D simulation governs LEV formation across all three wing geometries, validating the universality of the LESP concept for a given airfoil and Reynolds number
  • The pseudovortex ring is a mathematical construct ensuring zero vortex strength at the leading edge while maintaining the Kelvin condition across all three vortex sheets (bound, LEV, TEV)
  • The UVLM with LEV requires ~25 min on 1 processor vs ~1 day on 128 processors for RANS CFD — roughly three orders of magnitude faster, suitable for preliminary design and optimization loops
  • The excellent $C_M$ agreement but imperfect $C_L$ match suggests that the LEV model correctly captures vortex-induced pressure redistribution but underestimates the net suction force contribution at high angles of attack
  • Computational cost grows nonlinearly with simulation time due to increasing vortex element count; vortex amalgamation and deletion strategies from 2D methods need 3D extension for practical long-time simulations
  • The current work is a proof of concept; the nonuniform spanwise LEV shedding model is applicable to more advanced vortex formulations, making it a foundational contribution for future development

Conclusion

  • A UVLM augmented with a vortex-sheet LEV model has been formulated, extending the LESP concept from 2D to 3D finite wings for the first time
  • The method predicts spanwise LEV onset location and nonuniform growth patterns that correctly vary with wing geometry (unswept, tip-twisted, swept)
  • The pseudovortex ring construct provides a consistent mechanism for connecting the LEV sheet to the bound-vortex sheet while satisfying the Kelvin condition
  • LEV sheet shapes show broad qualitative agreement with iso-Q contours from RANS CFD across all three test cases
  • Pitching moment $C_M$ with LEV matches CFD excellently during the pitch-up phase; lift $C_L$ shows reasonable agreement with identified sources of discrepancy
  • Computational cost is approximately three orders of magnitude lower than RANS CFD, demonstrating potential for preliminary design applications
  • Future work requires higher-order discretization, improved time-stepping, vortex amalgamation strategies, and incorporation of tip vortex and trailing-edge separation models for routine use in long-time simulations
  • The nonuniform spanwise LEV shedding framework is transferable to advanced vortex methods, establishing this work as an important foundational step
