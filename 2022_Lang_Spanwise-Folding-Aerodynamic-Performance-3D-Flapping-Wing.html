Overview

This paper numerically investigates the effect of spanwise folding motion on the aerodynamic performance of a three-dimensional two-section flapping flat plate using CFD (ANSYS Fluent, $k$-$\omega$ SST turbulence model) at $\text{Re} = 69\,000$. Three folding kinematic parameters — folding amplitude ($\beta_m$), phase angle ($\varphi$), and mean folding angle ($\beta_0$) — are systematically varied under a sinusoidal flapping motion ($\Psi_m = 30^\circ$, $f = 5\,\text{Hz}$). The study establishes quantitative relationships between folding kinematics and aerodynamic forces through horizontal projection area, local tangential velocity, and effective angle of attack. A key finding is that a negative mean folding angle significantly increases time-averaged lift, consistent with natural bird flight and suggesting design guidance for bio-inspired micro-air vehicles.

Introduction

Motivation

  • Bird flight involves complex multi-degree-of-freedom wing motions including flapping, pitching, twisting, and spanwise folding, yet the aerodynamic mechanism of coupled flapping-folding motion remains insufficiently understood.
  • Observations of large birds (albatross, gull, stork) reveal prominent spanwise folding during flight, distinct from the flexion-extension seen in smaller birds.
  • Understanding folding kinematics is essential for designing bio-inspired micro-air vehicles with improved aerodynamic performance.

State of the Art

  • Han (2009) found that flapping-folding seagull wing motion shows lift advantage over pure flapping using CFD.
  • Karimian and Jahanbin (2020) established a quasi-steady model showing phase and amplitude of folding significantly affect two-section wing performance.
  • Verstraete et al. (2015) adopted UVLM to simulate seagull wing folding; later studied boundary flutter of two-section wings.
  • Experimental work by Ryu et al., Chen and Yeh, and Qin et al. confirmed benefits of folding for force generation.

Gap & Contribution

  • Prior studies lack systematic parametric investigation of folding amplitude, phase angle, and mean folding angle and their coupled effects on aerodynamic performance.
  • This paper provides a comprehensive CFD-based parametric study linking folding kinematics to aerodynamic forces through horizontal projection area, tangential velocity, effective angle of attack, and LEV dynamics.

Materials and Methods

Problem Setup

  • 3D two-section flat plate: chord $c = 100\,\text{mm}$, inner and outer wing lengths $L_1 = L_2 = 250\,\text{mm}$, cross-section thickness $2\%c$ with rounded edges.
  • Flapping: $\Psi(t) = \Psi_m \cos(2\pi f t)$, $\Psi_m = 30^\circ$, $f = 5\,\text{Hz}$; folding: $\beta(t) = \beta_0 + \beta_m \cos(2\pi f t + \varphi)$.
  • Freestream $U_\infty = 10\,\text{m/s}$; $\text{Re} = 69\,000$; gap root cutout of $0.2c$.

Governing Equations

  • Navier-Stokes equations solved with $k$-$\omega$ SST turbulence model (ANSYS Fluent); wing motion via UDF with DEFINE_GRID_MOTION macro.
  • Inner wing coordinate transformation: $\mathbf{r}'_{\text{inner}} = \mathbf{T}_2 \mathbf{R}_1 \mathbf{T}_1 \, \mathbf{r}_{0,\text{inner}}$ (translation + flapping rotation).
  • Outer wing transformation: $\mathbf{r}'_{\text{outer}} = \mathbf{T}_4 \mathbf{R}_2 \mathbf{T}_3 \mathbf{R}_1 \, \mathbf{r}_{0,\text{outer}}$ (flapping + folding rotation).
  • Horizontal projection area: $S = [L_1 \cos\Psi + L_2 \cos(\Psi + \beta)] \cdot c$.
  • Effective angle of attack: $\alpha_{\text{eff}} = \arctan(-V_{\text{sec}} / U_\infty)$.

Numerical Approach

  • Stationary zone ($90c \times 50c \times 60c$, Cartesian) + deformable zone ($21c \times 13c \times 20c$, tetrahedral); diffusion smoothing for dynamic mesh.
  • Second-order upwind; SIMPLEC pressure-velocity coupling; time step $T/200$; 6-cycle simulation.
  • Mesh independence: three resolutions ($1.5$, $3$, $6 \times 10^6$ cells); time step independence: 100, 200, 400 steps/period.

Validation

  • Validated against Heathcote et al. (2008) experiment: NACA0012 wing in pure heave, $k = 1.82$; thrust coefficient in good agreement.
  • Results closely matched numerical results by Liu (2009); small peak/valley discrepancy attributed to slight wing flexibility in experiment.

Results

Folding Amplitude ($\beta_m = -20^\circ$ to $+20^\circ$)

  • Introduces significant phase shift in lift and drag curves; peak lift magnitude remains similar across amplitudes.
  • Larger positive $\beta_m$ generates more negative drag (thrust) around mid-downstroke and mid-upstroke.
  • Horizontal projection area shows corresponding phase shift, confirming it dominates the lift trend.

Phase Angle ($\varphi = 70^\circ$ to $110^\circ$)

  • Controls force amplitude without phase shift: $\varphi = 70^\circ$ yields highest peak lift and strongest LEV suction.
  • Increasing $\varphi$ reduces lift amplitude and weakens thrust generation; time-averaged drag decreases with lower $\varphi$.
  • Lower $\varphi$ produces larger peak $\alpha_{\text{eff}}$, faster growth rates, and stronger outer-wing LEV development.

Mean Folding Angle ($\beta_0 = -20^\circ$ to $+20^\circ$)

  • Negative $\beta_0$ creates asymmetric forces: enhanced downstroke lift, reduced upstroke negative lift, significantly increasing mean lift coefficient.
  • Negative $\beta_0$ also generates more downstroke thrust but more upstroke drag, with slight change in time-averaged drag.
  • The result confirms natural birds' negative mean folding angle as beneficial for flight performance.

Flow Structure Analysis

  • Conical LEV grows along span; detaches at mid-span into arch-shaped structure; reattaches near tip due to tip vortex downwash.
  • Folding primarily affects the outer wing LEV: higher tangential velocity → faster LEV growth → larger circulation → stronger lift.
  • Inner wing LEV remains largely unaffected by folding parameters.
  • Dimensionless LEV circulation correlates with local tangential velocity along the span.

Results Interpretation

Mechanism Decomposition

  • Three kinematic parameters influence forces through distinct mechanisms: amplitude → phase shift, phase angle → amplitude control, mean angle → asymmetry.
  • Horizontal projection area provides first-order lift prediction; effective angle of attack and LEV dynamics explain instantaneous magnitudes.
  • Local tangential velocity $V_{\text{sec}}$ is the fundamental kinematic quantity linking folding parameters to LEV strength and force generation.

Biological Relevance

  • The finding that negative $\beta_0$ enhances time-averaged lift is consistent with Liu et al. observations of negative mean folding angles in forward-flying birds.
  • Suggests an evolutionary adaptation for efficient lift generation that can be mimicked in MAV design.

Design Implications

  • Phase angle selection involves a trade-off: smaller $\varphi$ enhances thrust but amplifies lift oscillation, with potential structural implications.
  • Arch-shaped LEV detachment and tip reattachment demonstrate fundamentally different vortex dynamics in 3D folding wings compared to 2D models.
  • The $k$-$\omega$ SST model at $\text{Re} = 69\,000$ captures essential LEV physics, though wing flexibility effects are not addressed.

Conclusion

Principal Findings

  • Spanwise folding motion significantly affects 3D flapping wing aerodynamics by modifying horizontal projection area, local tangential velocity, and effective angle of attack.
  • Folding amplitude causes phase shift in forces and enhances thrust at larger positive values.
  • Phase angle controls force amplitude: smaller $\varphi$ yields larger lift peaks, stronger LEV, and more thrust, but increases oscillation.
  • Negative mean folding angle significantly increases time-averaged lift through downstroke-upstroke asymmetry, confirming an advantage of natural bird folding kinematics.

Broader Impact

  • The LEV on the outer wing is the primary flow structure affected by folding; its arch-shaped detachment pattern is governed by local tangential velocity determined by folding kinematics.
  • These findings provide guidance for bio-inspired MAV design with actively controlled spanwise folding, particularly using negative mean folding angle for lift enhancement.
