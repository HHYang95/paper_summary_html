Overview

This paper presents a hybrid non-linear unsteady vortex lattice method coupled with the vortex particle method (NL UVLM-VPM) for aerodynamic simulation of rotor blades hovering in and out of ground effect. The UVLM models blade surfaces with vortex panels that are converted to Lagrangian vortex particles for the wake, eliminating panel stretching instabilities near the ground. A Vreman SGS model provides LES-based viscous diffusion via particle strength exchange (PSE), while the fast-multipole method (FMM) reduces computational complexity to O(N). Non-linear aerodynamics are achieved through viscous-inviscid alpha coupling with stripwise 2D RANS or empirical databases. Validated on the S-76 rotor from the AIAA Hover Prediction Workshop, the method achieves accuracy competitive with 3D URANS codes at drastically reduced cost. For Dr. Yang's research, this hybrid framework is relevant as a computationally efficient alternative to full CFD for rotorcraft and ornithopter wake modeling.

Introduction

Motivation

  • Computational rotary-wing aerodynamics is challenging due to intrinsic 3D unsteady rotor-wake interactions requiring many revolutions to reach steady-state forces
  • High-fidelity 3D URANS/DES require enormous resources (e.g., 5 days on 240–512 cores for 15–18 rotations on 40–88M cell meshes)
  • Numerical dissipation in Eulerian CFD destroys wake vorticity, requiring excessively fine grids to preserve vortex structures
  • Panel-based free-wake methods suffer from unrealistic wake deformations when filaments are convected in diverging directions, especially in ground effect

State of the Art

  • UVLM with panelized wakes has been applied to hovering rotors but struggles with wake panel stretching near the ground
  • Vortex particle methods (VPM) offer mesh-free Lagrangian wake representations without connectivity constraints
  • LES-based VPM with Smagorinsky SGS has been applied to turbulent flows and rotor wakes
  • Non-linear alpha and circulation coupling methods developed for fixed wings but only preliminary rotorcraft work existed

Gap & Contribution

  • No prior method combined NL UVLM + VPM + LES-SGS + FMM for rotor simulations in ground effect
  • The Vreman SGS model had not been applied to mesh-free VPM for rotorcraft wakes
  • The viscous-inviscid alpha coupling framework is extended from fixed-wing to rotary-wing aerodynamics

Methodology

UVLM Formulation

  • Lifting surfaces modeled by vortex panels; flow tangency enforced at collocation points via linear system AΓ = −u∞·n
  • Induced velocity from straight-line vortex elements via Biot-Savart law with Vatistas smoothing kernel to regularize singularities
  • Time stepping: blade displacement per rotational velocity, wake panel shedding from trailing edge, free-wake convection

Lagrangian Vortex Particle Method

  • 3D vortex-velocity equations in Lagrangian frame with vortex stretching and viscous diffusion terms
  • Vorticity approximated by particles with 3D Gaussian smoothing; transpose stretching scheme conserves total vorticity
  • Time integration via 3rd-order low-storage Runge-Kutta

LES with Vreman SGS

  • Particle strength exchange (PSE) discretizes viscous diffusion directly in the vortex strength equation
  • Vreman SGS model guarantees zero eddy viscosity for zero SGS dissipation, appropriate transitional/near-wall behavior
  • More accurate than standard Smagorinsky for inhomogeneous LES, as accurate as dynamic Smagorinsky

Hybrid Conversion

  • Trailing and shed vortex lines converted to equivalent vortex particles: Γp = ΔΓ dl/n
  • Upstream vortex lines retained to prevent circulation jump at trailing edge
  • Four interaction types (particle-panel, particle-particle, panel-particle, panel-panel) for induced velocity and gradient

Non-Linear Alpha Coupling

  • Modified Van Dam alpha method (Gallay formulation) iteratively corrects angles of attack using 2D viscous databases
  • Convergence: |Cl,inv − Cl,vis| ≤ 10−4; two databases tested: Bousman correlations and 2D RANS (Spalart-Allmaras)

FMM & Ground Modeling

  • Fast-multipole method reduces complexity from O(N2) to O(N); adapted for mirror-image ground boundary conditions
  • Ground modeled via mirror image method: reflected particles included in FMM for no-penetration condition

Key Results

Out of Ground Effect (S-76, Mtip=0.65)

  • Mesh convergence: 8×20 panels, ΔΨ=2.5° selected; finest mesh (16×40) ran 42 days on 40 cores reaching only 15.7 rotations with similar converged results
  • Thrust: NL UVLM-VPM with 2D RANS achieves median CT error of 4.03% vs experiment; linear UVLM-VPM underpredicts due to missing compressibility
  • Torque: NL coupling dramatically improves CQ by including viscous and pressure drag from databases
  • Figure of merit: NL UVLM-VPM (RANS) achieves mean FM error 5.98%, competitive with 3D URANS-Lagrangian codes; linear UVLM-VPM has ~73% FM error
  • Spanwise loading: Thrust and torque distributions at CT/σ=0.09 agree well with higher-fidelity codes, especially hybrid RANS-Lagrangian methods
  • Pressure coefficient: Cp at r/R=0.6, 0.85, 0.975 agrees well; differences increase near tip

In Ground Effect (z/R=1.2, Mtip=0.6)

  • LES eddy viscosity appropriately high in starting vortex, small near rotor (preserving tip vortex features)
  • Thrust increase in ground effect follows experimental trends better than Cheeseman (1955) theory at close distances
  • NL coupling slightly overpredicts CT but provides best torque and figure of merit predictions
  • Computational cost: ~3 days on 40 cores (C++, OpenMP) vs. 5 days on 240–512 cores for 3D URANS

Discussion & Implications

Key Findings

  • The hybrid formulation addresses three major limitations of prior potential-flow methods: wake stretching in ground effect, absence of viscous/compressibility effects, and O(N2) computational cost
  • The alpha coupling proves more robust than circulation coupling for rotary-wing applications, converging reliably across all collective angles
  • The Vreman SGS provides appropriate LES stabilization without excessive dissipation of coherent wake structures

Strengths

  • Orders-of-magnitude computational savings compared to 3D URANS with comparable accuracy for hover predictions
  • Modular viscous database: any 2D aerodynamic effect can be incorporated through sectional data without code changes
  • Mesh-free wake eliminates background mesh refinement requirements for vorticity preservation

Limitations

  • No 3D rotational augmentation (centrifugal pumping, Coriolis stall delay) in viscous databases
  • Thrust overprediction at low collectives where linear UVLM-VPM already matches experiments
  • Tip region loading peak too narrow compared to 3D URANS due to panel discretization
  • IGE validation limited to experimental data only (no higher-fidelity code comparisons)

Relevance to Ornithopter Research

  • The NL UVLM-VPM framework offers a computationally tractable model for ornithopter simulations where unsteady wake interactions dominate
  • The viscous-inviscid coupling is applicable to flapping-wing UVLM where Reynolds-dependent viscous effects must be captured without full 3D CFD
  • FMM acceleration enables practical multi-stroke simulations of ornithopter flight

Conclusion & Future Work

Conclusion

  • The hybrid NL UVLM-VPM with Vreman LES-SGS and FMM acceleration successfully simulates rotor hover in and out of ground effect with accuracy competitive to 3D URANS at a fraction of the cost
  • The non-linear alpha coupling greatly improves torque and figure of merit predictions by incorporating viscous and compressibility effects through 2D databases
